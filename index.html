Interactive Pyxis

Demystify the cabinet you'll meet on day one of your rotation.

  • Click-to-explore drawers for controlled meds, supplies, airway gear, and lines.
  • Vaporizer panel: sevo, iso, and des side-by-side with teaching context.
  • Curated drug cards with mechanism, dosing, and "what to look for" pearls.
  • Ask AI chat to demystify any drug or device on the spot.

Interactive Ventilator

Why this exists

Open-sourcing anesthesia education

Anesthesia is hard to see. The OR moves fast, decisions live inside the attending's head, and a textbook can't show you a vent waveform shifting under your changes. Anesthesia Playground is a small attempt to fix that: a public, free, modern sandbox that pairs interactive simulations with AI tutoring so any student, anywhere, can practice the way faculty already think.

  • OpenFree for learners. Free for institutions. Built to be borrowed.
  • Interactive Every screen is a thing you can poke, not a slide.
  • AI-augmentedCoaching, debriefs, and case generation that scale a teaching attending's bandwidth.

Built to grow a community of anesthesia trainees and educators around digital tech × medical education.

⚕ Educational Sources and Disclaimer

Educational Resource — Not for Clinical Decision-Making

This tool is designed exclusively for medical students on anesthesiology rotations to build context around what they observe in the OR. The information presented, including drug doses, mechanisms, and clinical pearls, is meant to supplement direct teaching, not replace it. It should never be relied upon as the sole basis for patient care decisions. Always conduct your own research, defer to your supervising attendings, follow local protocols and formularies, and exercise clinical judgment adapted to each patient.

Informed by faculty, ASA/AHA/ACC guidelines, FDA labels, peer-reviewed literature, Deranged Physiology, and Manual of Clinical Anesthesiology by Chu, Traynor, Kurup.
